## Three dimensions of color: Hue, Saturation, Value {.unnumbered}

Map makers can manipulate all three of these dimensions on maps to convey
different messages. 

When choosing how to display color and which dimensions of color to utilize, always consider: 

* Will people with colorblindness be able to interpret your map?

* Are you displaying different categorical variables, or differences in intensity of quantitative data?

* What may people interpret or associate with your color choices? e.g. bright reds and oranges are often associated with a subjective view of something "bad." 

* Is the use of color critical to understanding or interpretting the map? Could we consider using a consistent grey scale instead?

wavelength on the electromagnetic spectrum. In other words: moving from orange, to
yellow, to green, to blue, to indigo, etc. 

**VALUE**  is related to light versus dark. It applies to hues, as well as shades of gray. 

**SATURATION**, sometimes called "chroma", refers to the intensity of a color. 
**Gray tones do not have saturation.** So down here, on the left, you're seeing
this purple rectangle that's showing movement from this very light, not intense
purple, all the way to the high end, a very intense purple color.

    + This color scale would also be impossible for someone who is red-green colorblind to interpret

* Use **hue** for differences in category: best for **qualitative** data
